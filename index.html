My Story

Hi, my name is Sibusiso Brain Nzama. I am a 17-year-old science student studying Physical Sciences and Life Sciences.

I am a self-taught software developer who is still learning, exploring, and building projects every day. What makes my journey different is that I create and learn using only a mobile phone. Despite these limitations, I stay committed to improving my skills and understanding how technology works.

My passion for technology started early. At the age of 14, I built my own functional portable charger using old electronic components at home. That experience taught me how to think creatively, solve problems with limited resources, and build something useful from what I had. Since then, I have continued to explore and experiment with different ideas.

Beyond software development, I am also passionate about art, writing, and chess. These interests help shape the way I think. Art allows me to express creativity visually, writing helps me organize and communicate ideas, and chess strengthens my ability to think strategically and make decisions.

I am also deeply interested in philosophy, which I started studying at the age of 16. It changed the way I see the world and how I approach challenges in life. One of the ideas I developed for myself is a personal philosophy of staying positive no matter the situation. This mindset helps me stay focused, keep learning, and continue building even when things are difficult.

So far, I have built several small projects, including an AI logic analyser that compares two things and decides a winner using reasoning, a mini arcade-style game, a calculator, and a video downloader. Each project represents my progress and my willingness to learn by doing.

My goal is to continue growing as a developer, combining technology, creativity, and logical thinking to build more advanced and meaningful projects. I believe that with the right tools and opportunities, I can take my skills to a much higher level.

At the moment, I am working with limited resources, but I am fully committed to improving every day. If you believe in my journey and would like to support my progress, your support will help me gain better tools and continue building, learning, and creating.
